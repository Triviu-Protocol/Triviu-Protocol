Lesson 05 · Wallet & key safety

The safest thing about Triviu is what it cannot do.

Triviu never holds your funds and has no way to ask for them. That removes a whole class of risk — but not the ones that actually drain wallets. Those live on your side of the screen, and they are the same three habits that keep any self-custodial user safe.

Your key stays with you

Triviu is non-custodial by construction. Your key signs transactions locally and never leaves your machine. The executor is stateless and has no deposit function — there is no balance of user funds anywhere in the protocol for anyone to take. The one place you can put value, the Gas-Tank, is a per-user reserve where only you can withdraw your own balance, verified reentrancy-safe. Nothing leaves it except back to the account that funded it.

The real defenses are yours

Because the protocol can't be the attack surface, the surface that matters is your wallet hygiene. Three habits do most of the work:

01
Approvals hygiene
Token approve allowances persist after a trade. Grant the minimum, and revoke stale ones — a forgotten unlimited approval to a bad contract is how most "I didn't sign anything" drains actually happen.
02
A hardware wallet
Keep the signing key off any internet-connected machine. It signs when you confirm on the device and is never exposed to a compromised browser or clipboard.
03
Read exactly what you sign
Confirm the actual transaction — the contract, the function, the amounts — not the marketing around it. If a prompt doesn't match what you meant to do, reject it.

Verify, don't trust

The Executor, Registry and Gas-Tank are verified on the block explorer: anyone reads the exact deployed bytecode, not a description of it. Read it, or read someone's review of it, before you run. And the honest caveat that belongs on this page: review reduces contract risk, it does not bring it to zero — the known limitations are documented openly in the audits and the Tradeoff Records. Nobody selling you certainty is telling the truth; the code, in the open, is the only thing that is.

Do it now Open your wallet's approvals screen and revoke anything you don't recognize. It costs a little gas and closes the most common drain — no Triviu account needed.
