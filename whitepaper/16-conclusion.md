# 16. Conclusion

We have described an arbitrage and education infrastructure that does not depend
on trust. Every rule is public. Every execution is verifiable. Every parameter
has a history. Every fee is charged on-chain, only on profit, and capped in
bytecode. And every limitation is documented with the same prominence as every
capability — including the one limitation the industry hides, that for an
individual the expected result is not a gain.

Bitcoin's insight was that you do not need to trust a third party if you can
verify the chain. Triviu applies that insight to a corner of DeFi that had drifted
back to the old model — closed bots, unverifiable results, promises where there
should be proofs. The answer is not a better promise. It is the removal of the
promise, and the publication of everything a promise would have concealed: the
code, the math, the audit, and the odds.

What Triviu offers is not an outcome. It is the ability to verify one — including
the ability to verify that the outcome is unlikely, and to walk away better
educated for having checked. A protocol that teaches you to read the contract
must also teach you to read the market, and this document has tried to do both.

The mark is three equal nodes in a cycle, none above the others, the flow ending
where it began. It is not a hierarchy and it is not a promise. It is a claim that
can be checked — which is the only kind of claim Triviu makes.

> *Don't trust. Verify.*
