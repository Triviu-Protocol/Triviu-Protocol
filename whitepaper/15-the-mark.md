# 15. The mark — "The Cycle"

A protocol that asks the world to *verify rather than trust* cannot wear a logo
that asks to be trusted. The Triviu mark was designed as an argument, not a
decoration: every property of it can be read back to something the protocol
actually does.

  <img src="assets/simbolo.svg" width="180" alt="The Triviu mark: three equal nodes joined by directional arcs in an A→B→C→A cycle"/>
</p>

Three equal nodes joined by directional arcs, rotated so **no side rests on the
ground** and **no node sits on top**. A pyramid is static hierarchy — someone
above, many below; the very silhouette a financial-pyramid scam wears. This is
the opposite: a **cycle** that ends where it began, three peers and a flow, no
apex to receive and no base to sustain. The form is the whitepaper's argument in a
single figure.

## Three readings, one mark

The mark is legible three ways at once, and each reading corresponds to a real
layer of the project.

| Reading | What the three nodes are | Where it comes from |
|---|---|---|
| **The trivium** | Three roads that meet | The name — Latin *trivium*, the classical curriculum that founded all education |
| **The route** | Three pools in the cycle A → B → C → A | The product — the atomic triangular arbitrage |
| **The trilemma** | Decentralization · Security · Scalability | The context — every blockchain's fundamental constraint |

A mark of three, with three readings. The third one — the
[trilemma](13-trilemma.md) — structures the entire brand.

## Color as taxonomy

The palette is a proof-plate triad — blue, red and yellow over paper and ink, in
the tradition of the 19th-century books that set out to *prove*, not to persuade
(Byrne's *Elements of Euclid*, 1847). In Triviu each color carries a meaning:
**each color is one axis of the trilemma.**

| Node | Token | Hex | Axis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Top-left | Ultramar | `#2743C7` | Decentralization |
| Right | Lacre | `#C13327` | Security |
| Bottom-left | Açafrão | `#E8B23A` | Scalability |

None of them is "profit green" — that is deliberate. Content about governance
accents Ultramar; about audits and verification, Lacre; about gas and cost,
Açafrão. One surface, one axis; the full triad appears only in the mark itself.

## The rule of 120°

The mark rotates only in multiples of **120°**, always in the A→B→C→A direction —
one node of the cycle at a time. Nothing bounces, nothing pulses: hype in motion
is still hype. The motion is the cycle advancing, never decoration.

## Typography — three open voices

Three type families, all open-source, echoing the rule of three:

- **Libre Caslon Text** — the letterform of the proof books, for display and
  quotations. The tradition of demonstration, not advertisement.
- **Public Sans** — created as a government public typeface, maintained in the
  open. Its name carries what Triviu stands for: *what is public can be verified.*
- **IBM Plex Mono** — everything on-chain is set in mono: addresses, hashes,
  blocks, parameters. Truncation in the `0x1234…abcd` form.

## Two mandatory components

The brand is enforced, not suggested. Two components may never be removed:

1. The **risk notice** — present wherever execution is discussed.
2. The **"AI-GENERATED" label** — present on all AI-generated content.

Forks and integrations that strip the risk notice, the AI label or the tradeoff
records may not use the Triviu name. The mark is a promise about honesty; remove
the honesty and you have removed the mark.
